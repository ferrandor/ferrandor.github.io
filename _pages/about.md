I am a PhD candidate in the Graduate Interdisciplinary Program in Applied Mathematics at the University of Arizona. I work with Dr. Michael (Misha) Chertkov on using tools from probability, statistics, and optimization to operate power grids at different spatial and temporal scales, and under uncertainty from both renewable and conventional generator technologies. One major focus of this research is to use physics-informed machine learning to enhance current optimization methodologies. In this vein, I am broadly interested in both theoretical and practical aspects of scientific machine learning. I also work on risk-sensitive stochastic optimal control theory, with most applications of interest drawn from energy systems.
