Hello! I am a fifth-year PhD candidate in the [Graduate Interdisciplinary Program in Applied Mathematics](https://appliedmath.arizona.edu/) at the [University of Arizona](https://www.arizona.edu/). My research focuses on **data-driven modeling and optimization of energy systems**. In particular, I seek to synthesize classical theory of optimization, stochastic processes, and graphs and networks with modern artificial intelligence tools to develop efficient, reliable, and interpretable algorithms for operating power and gas systems. I'm particularly interested in improving the robustness and resilience of critical infrastructure systems in the presence of extreme events. My dissertation advisor is [Michael (Misha) Chertkov](https://sites.arizona.edu/misha-chertkov/). 

Outside of work, I enjoy spending time with family, reading historical fiction, and learning as much as I can about tropical meteorology, Linux computing, and classic rock music.
