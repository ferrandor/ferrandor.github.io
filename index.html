Robert Ferrando

I am a postdoctoral research associate in the Information Systems and Modeling Group at Los Alamos National Laboratory. I received my PhD in Applied Mathematics from the University of Arizona in August, 2025, under the advisement of Misha Chertkov. My doctoral thesis was on Physics-Informed Artificial Intelligence for Multi-Scale Energy Systems.

Research Interests

I have broad interests in interdisciplinary applied and computational mathematics applied to energy systems. Some of my recent projects involve:

(1.) Physics-informed machine learning for real-time electricity market clearing.
(2.) Mixed-integer nonlinear programming for operation of power transmission and distribution grids.
(3.) Data-driven stochastic control of interconnected power and natural gas grids.
(4.) Bayesian surrogate modeling for stochastic natural gas dynamics.

My mathematical training is primarily in optimization, numerical methods, and applied probability and statistics. Computer programming is a critical part of my day-to-day work. I mostly use the Julia programming language. I consider myself an application-driven programmer, and I am always striving to improve my coding workflow.

Earlier Biographical Information

Prior to beginning graduate school, I received a BS in Mathematics from the Macaulay Honors College at CUNY, College of Staten Island (CSI). While at CSI, I worked with Stephen Wollman on kinetic equations of plasma physics. Following graduation, I was also an adjunct lecturer at CSI, where I taught introductory undergraduate courses in algebra, precalculus, and statistics.

Even prior to my undergraduate studies, I attended Staten Island Technical High School, where I was properly introduced to both the physical and social sciences.

When I am not working, my hobbies include listening to classic rock music, reading about current events, writing, and Linux computing. I also enjoy the outdoors and frequently utilize local hiking trails. My pronouns are he/him. I prefer to be addressed as "Robert" in professional correspondence.
